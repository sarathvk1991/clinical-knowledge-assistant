SECTION 1: HYPERTENSION MANAGEMENT

Definition:
Hypertension is defined as a sustained systolic blood pressure (SBP) ≥130 mmHg or diastolic blood pressure (DBP) ≥80 mmHg (ACC/AHA 2017 guidelines).

Classification:
- Normal: SBP <120 and DBP <80 mmHg
- Elevated: SBP 120–129 and DBP <80 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension: SBP 130–139 or DBP 80–89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: SBP ≥140 or DBP ≥90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: SBP >180 and/or DBP >120 mmHg

First-line Treatment:
- Lifestyle modifications: DASH diet, sodium restriction (<2.3 g/day), regular aerobic exercise (150 min/week), weight loss, smoking cessation
- Pharmacotherapy for Stage 1 with high CV risk or Stage 2:
  * Thiazide diuretics (e.g., chlorthalidone 12.5–25 mg/day)
  * ACE inhibitors (e.g., lisinopril 10–40 mg/day)
  * ARBs (e.g., losartan 50–100 mg/day)
  * Calcium channel blockers (e.g., amlodipine 5–10 mg/day)

Target BP Goals:
- General adults: <130/80 mmHg
- CKD or diabetes: <130/80 mmHg
- Age ≥65 years: SBP <130 mmHg

SECTION 2: TYPE 2 DIABETES MANAGEMENT

Diagnostic Criteria (ADA 2023):
- Fasting plasma glucose ≥126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
- 2-hour plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL during 75 g OGTT
- HbA1c ≥6.5% (48 mmol/mol)
- Random plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL with hyperglycemic symptoms

Glycemic Targets:
- HbA1c: <7.0% for most non-pregnant adults
- Preprandial glucose: 80–130 mg/dL
- Peak postprandial glucose (1–2 hr after meal): <180 mg/dL

First-line Pharmacotherapy:
- Metformin remains the preferred initial agent (if tolerated and no contraindications)
  * Start: 500 mg once or twice daily with meals
  * Target dose: 1000 mg twice daily
  * Contraindicated if eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73 m²

Second-line Options (add if HbA1c not at goal after 3 months):
- GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., semaglutide, liraglutide) — preferred with established CVD or high CV risk
- SGLT-2 inhibitors (e.g., empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) — preferred with heart failure or CKD
- DPP-4 inhibitors (e.g., sitagliptin) — weight neutral, well tolerated
- Basal insulin — when HbA1c markedly elevated or oral agents insufficient

Monitoring:
- HbA1c: every 3 months until stable, then every 6 months
- Annual: lipid panel, urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio, eGFR, foot exam, dilated eye exam

SECTION 3: COMMUNITY-ACQUIRED PNEUMONIA (CAP)

Diagnostic Criteria:
- New pulmonary infiltrate on chest imaging
- Plus ≥2 of: fever >38°C, leukocytosis or leukopenia, purulent sputum, cough/dyspnea/pleuritic chest pain

Severity Assessment (PSI/PORT Score or CURB-65):
CURB-65 criteria (1 point each):
- Confusion (new)
- Urea >7 mmol/L (BUN >20 mg/dL)
- Respiratory rate ≥30/min
- Blood pressure (SBP <90 or DBP ≤60 mmHg)
- Age ≥65 years

Score 0–1: Low severity → outpatient treatment
Score 2: Moderate severity → consider short inpatient stay
Score ≥3: High severity → hospital admission, consider ICU if ≥4–5

Antibiotic Recommendations:
Outpatient, no comorbidities:
- Amoxicillin 1 g three times daily for 5 days, OR
- Doxycycline 100 mg twice daily for 5 days

Outpatient, with comorbidities (COPD, diabetes, liver/renal disease, immunosuppression):
- Respiratory fluoroquinolone (levofloxacin 750 mg daily or moxifloxacin 400 mg daily), OR
- Beta-lactam + macrolide (amoxicillin-clavulanate + azithromycin)

Inpatient, non-ICU:
- Beta-lactam (ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV daily) + macrolide (azithromycin 500 mg daily), OR
- Respiratory fluoroquinolone monotherapy

Duration: 5 days (if clinically stable after 48–72 hours)

SECTION 4: ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME (ACS)

Types:
- ST-elevation MI (STEMI): Complete occlusion → emergent reperfusion
- Non-ST-elevation MI (NSTEMI): Partial occlusion
- Unstable angina: No biomarker elevation

Initial Management (all ACS):
- Aspirin 325 mg loading dose (then 81 mg daily)
- P2Y12 inhibitor (ticagrelor 180 mg loading or clopidogrel 600 mg loading)
- Anticoagulation (heparin, enoxaparin, or bivalirudin)
- High-intensity statin (atorvastatin 80 mg or rosuvastatin 40 mg)
- Beta-blocker (within 24 hours if no contraindication)
- ACE inhibitor or ARB (within 24 hours if EF ≤40%, hypertension, or diabetes)

Reperfusion Strategy for STEMI:
- Primary PCI: Preferred if door-to-balloon time <90 minutes (120 min if transferred)
- Fibrinolysis: If PCI not available within 120 min of first medical contact

SECTION 5: ANTIBIOTIC STEWARDSHIP PRINCIPLES

Core Principles:
1. Use the narrowest spectrum effective agent
2. Obtain cultures before starting antibiotics when possible
3. Review and de-escalate at 48–72 hours based on culture results
4. Use oral step-down therapy as soon as clinically appropriate
5. Adhere to evidence-based duration recommendations

Common Durations (evidence-based):
- CAP (outpatient): 5 days
- Uncomplicated UTI (women): 3–5 days (nitrofurantoin), 1 day (fosfomycin)
- Cellulitis: 5 days (extend if not improving)
- COPD exacerbation: 5 days
- Sinusitis (bacterial): 5–7 days
- Intra-abdominal infection (source controlled): 4 days
